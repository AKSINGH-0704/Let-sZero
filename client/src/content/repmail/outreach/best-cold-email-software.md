"Best cold email software" has no single answer, because these tools are not really the same product. Some bundle a leads database, some are built around unlimited mailboxes, some are multichannel suites priced per seat, and some are metered sending layers. The useful question is not which is best overall, but which model fits how you actually work. Below is an honest map of the field, with pricing verified in July 2026, so confirm current numbers on each vendor's site before buying.

## The five, and what each is really for

**Instantly** is a flat-rate sequencer with a built-in leads database. Its Outreach plans are Growth at $47/month and Hypergrowth at $97/month, with the leads database billed separately as Credits. It fits teams that want sending and data under one subscription.

**Smartlead** is built around unlimited connected mailboxes on every plan, priced Base $39, Pro $94, Unlimited Smart $174, and Unlimited Prime $379 per month. It is a favorite of high-volume senders and agencies, with a per-client white-label portal add-on.

**Lemlist** is a per-seat multichannel suite, Email Pro at $79 and Multichannel Expert at $109 per user per month, known for visual personalization and LinkedIn plus calling. Cost scales with headcount.

**Apollo** is primarily a B2B data platform with sending bundled in, priced per user at roughly $49, $79, and $119 per month annually, with data credits that expire each cycle. It is a data-first choice, with a three-seat minimum on its top tier.

**RepMail** is a pay-as-you-go sending layer on AWS SES. You buy credits, spend them on what you send, and they stay valid for six months. It focuses on native SES delivery, GPT-4o per-recipient personalization, and real-time bounce suppression, and it brings no built-in database.

## Match the model to your sending

The deciding factor is usually your sending pattern and stack, not a feature checklist. Steady, predictable volume rewards a flat subscription like Smartlead or Instantly. Variable or seasonal volume wastes less on consumption pricing like RepMail. Teams that need to license every member in a multichannel suite lean toward Lemlist's per-seat model. Teams whose central need is finding prospects start with Apollo's data and add a sending layer. And agencies juggling many client mailboxes often value Smartlead's unlimited-mailbox design or a metered per-client cost.

## The one thing no tool solves

Whichever you choose, the software sets your architecture, not your outcome. Inbox placement still comes down to authentication, sending pace, list quality, and content. A flat plan does not clean your list; unlimited mailboxes do not authenticate your domain; a big database does not stop bounces. The tools differ most in how much of that discipline they automate for you, real-time suppression, pre-send spam analysis, per-recipient personalization, which is where the comparison actually earns its keep. Pick the model that fits your work, then do the deliverability fundamentals regardless.
